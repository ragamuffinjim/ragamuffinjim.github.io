Welcome to my little slice of the web!

Quite a few years ago @lohang@micro.alochana.net and I were in a conversation somewhere in the Fediverse and the gist of the conversation was that "x, y, and z need to happen then social media will be a better place to be." I was in a rather snarky mood that day and said something akin to "what needs to happen is that everyone needs to go back to having personal websites, reading each other via RSS feeds, and communicating via email. Then the whole internet will be full of fairy dust and unicorn farts." Or at least something similar to that.

It took me a couple of years to reach the point where I finally got a gutfull of the parasocial networks and decided to do something about it. I ragequit a bunch of my social media accounts and I learned how to build a page on Github and began building what I thought was a a website.

Well I was a little wrong in what I was building. I was building a blog not a website. A blog is an online diary not a website. And I am the kind of guy who doesn't find the details of my life to be particularly interesting so I have no clue why anyone else would want to read what I was writing. So after the initial excitement of building the site and posting a few items this site fell into the doldrums and pretty much ceased to exist.

Somewhere along the line I bumped into How Blogs Broke the Web and that opened up a whole slew of thoughts and ideas and led me to a concept known as digital gardens. The idea is not new at all. It is the way the internet was back in the early days of the worldwideweb. Goodness, back then we all had pages on Geocities and other places of similar ilk. Social media conformity? We never heard of that back then. We all did out own thing and we did it proudly. If you didn't like a site you didn't have to go look at it.

Oh how I miss those days!

Well, long story longer, I have decided that this "blog" site is going the way of the dodo. Sure, there will be a "blog" section but I am more interested in creating a small space online that is distinctly me and about stuff that interests me. I am tired of being told what to think and what to like.

There will be a bunch of different topics. I make no promises as to what will be here. I can tell you there will be a "tech" section, and a blog. I will probably include some stuff about my woodworking. Who knows? That is the fun of a digital garden, it is about everything and nothing all at once.

Some small details
